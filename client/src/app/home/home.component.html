About Hari-katha and Classical Music

"Hari-Katha Kalakshepam" is a an art form of story telling which blends the tradition of music and speech together, its mainly relevant to Hindu Gods and Hindu mythology. Only a talented person can manage this art form as he/she has to be a singer, actor and an orator at the same time. This form is also known as Harikatha, Kathakalashepam and according to historians, Mahabharata and Ramayana were initially performed orally in the form of Harikatha.
Kathaprasangam in Kerala has been influenced by art forms like Chakyarkoothu and Padakam. Sathakatha Kalashepam, is also similar art form that can be seen in many folk and cultural traditions in different languages in different parts of India.

"Classical Carnatic Music" - Carnatic music, Karnāṭaka saṃgīta, or Karnāṭaka saṅgītam, is a system of music commonly associated with southern India, where the basic elements such as Shruti (the relative musical pitch), Swara (the musical sound of a single note), Raaga (the mode or melodic formulæ), and Thaalam (the rhythmic cycles) form the foundation. It is mostly relevant to Hindu Gods and devotion to the lord through music.
The greatest concentration of Carnatic musicians is to be found in the city of Chennai, India.
